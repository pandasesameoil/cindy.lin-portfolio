## Overview

At Sunstone Management VC, I had the opportunity to work across startup ecosystem operations, partnerships, investor relations, event branding, and venture community building throughout Southern California. Through this experience, I became increasingly interested in how startup ecosystems are built — not only through capital, but through relationships, recurring interactions, and long-term community infrastructure.

## Event Operations & Community Growth

During my time at Sunstone, I helped support and coordinate more than **80** startup ecosystem events, including weekly founder demo days, LA Tech Week events, CES founder gatherings, NVIDIA GTC side events, Irvine Tech Day, private investor networking events, and community innovation activations. Across these initiatives, the ecosystem reached more than **40,000** community members and showcased **70+** startups through over **40** founder pitch sessions.

One of the most meaningful aspects of this experience was realizing that ecosystem building is rarely about a single event. Instead, it is built through consistency.

Every week through Sunstone Stage Connects, founders pitched their startups, investors exchanged ideas, and new relationships formed through recurring community programming. Behind each event was a significant amount of coordination — founder outreach, investor communication, sponsorship management, partnership alignment, venue logistics, branding design, and real-time operational problem solving.

One of the largest initiatives I supported was Irvine Dream Run, where participation grew from **300** to **1,200** attendees across **three** annual events through sponsorship outreach, partnership coordination, and community-led marketing efforts. I also helped coordinate more than **38** ecosystem partnerships for Irvine Tech Day across universities, startup organizations, chambers of commerce, local governments, and innovation communities.

## Investor Relations & Operations

Beyond community operations, I also gained exposure to venture operations and investor relations by supporting quarterly investor updates across **16** active funds and more than **100** portfolio companies within a **$30M** AUM environment. This included managing Airtable-based relationship tracking systems, organizing portfolio reporting workflows, and supporting investor communications.

Being close to both founders and investors gave me a broader perspective on how venture firms operate not only as investors, but also as ecosystem platforms that help startups gain visibility, relationships, and long-term strategic support.

## Cross-Border Initiatives

I was also fortunate to observe cross-border startup collaboration firsthand. One project involved supporting discussions surrounding a potential investment opportunity for an artificial heart startup by helping connect an international medical device industrialist with a U.S.-based R&D team for manufacturing and commercialization conversations. Experiences like this expanded my understanding of how innovation ecosystems increasingly operate across industries, geographies, and specialized networks.

## Key Learnings

In addition to operations and partnerships, I also contributed to event branding and visual storytelling across multiple startup ecosystem initiatives. I designed event graphics, promotional materials, and visual assets used across founder demo days, investor gatherings, and ecosystem activations.

What continues to motivate me most is the idea that startup ecosystems are deeply collaborative environments. Behind every successful founder story is usually a broader network of operators, investors, organizers, and partners helping create opportunities for innovation to grow.

Long term, I hope to continue building at the intersection of startup ecosystems, partnerships, venture communities, and innovation platforms — helping founders and organizations create environments where ambitious ideas can scale more effectively together.
